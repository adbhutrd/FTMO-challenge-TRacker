How to Pass Your FTMO Challenge on the First Attempt

After analyzing hundreds of successful FTMO challenges, here are the strategies that separate first-time passers from repeat takers.

Key Stat: Over 60% of FTMO challenges fail. Most failures come from rule violations, not trading losses.

1. Master the Rules Before You Trade

Don't start trading until you can explain every rule from memory. The most common failures come from rules traders didn't fully understand.

2. Use Automated Rule Tracking

Manual tracking leads to errors. Use a tool that automatically calculates drawdown from peak balance, tracks profit target progress, and monitors daily loss limits. Our free FTMO tracker does all of this.

3. Start with Small Positions

Your first few days should be conservative. Build a buffer of 2-3% before increasing position sizes. This gives you room to handle drawdown later.

4. Track Drawdown from Day One

As soon as you have a profitable day, your peak balance increases. Track drawdown from peak from the very first trade.

5. Plan Your Daily Loss Limit

Never risk more than half your daily loss limit in a single trade. If your limit is $2,500, keep max risk per trade under $1,250.

6. Watch the Best Day Rule (1-Step)

If you have one exceptional day, you may need significantly more total profit to pass. Keep your best day under 40% of total target if possible.

7. Don't Rush Minimum Days

2-Step requires 4 days minimum per phase. Spreading your trades over more days reduces variance and gives you more data to analyze.

8. Track Everything

Log every trade, every balance change, every rule check. Our free tracker does this automatically.

9. Use the Telegram Bot

Track your challenge from your phone using @ArdTradingBot. Add trades via Telegram and check status anytime.

10. Review and Adjust

After each trading day, review your performance. Look for patterns in your winners and losers. Adjust your strategy accordingly.
