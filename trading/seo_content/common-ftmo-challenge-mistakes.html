10 Common FTMO Challenge Mistakes

After 3 years of FTMO challenges and mentoring other traders, here are the most common mistakes I see — and how to avoid them.

1. Miscalculating Drawdown

Using starting balance instead of peak balance. This is the #1 mistake.

Fix: Use a tracker that automatically calculates from peak.

2. Ignoring the Best Day Rule

One huge day means you need double that day's profit before passing. Many traders don't realize this until it's too late.

3. Forgetting Minimum Trading Days

Hitting the profit target in 2 days doesn't mean you pass — you still need 4 minimum trading days for 2-Step.

4. Misunderstanding Daily Loss Limits

The daily loss limit is based on the PREVIOUS day's balance, not your starting balance. This means your daily limit changes as your balance changes.

5. Overtrading After a Win

Getting overconfident after hitting 8% profit and taking excessive risks. The challenge isn't over until it's verified.

6. Not Having a Risk Management Plan

Trading without predetermined position sizes, stop losses, and daily limits. This is a recipe for disaster.

7. Ignoring Phase 2 Differences

Phase 2 has the same risk limits but half the target. Many traders fail Phase 2 because they rush or get complacent.

8. Emotional Trading After Drawdown

Taking bigger risks to recover from drawdown, which usually makes things worse.

9. Not Using Available Tools

There are free tools like our FTMO tracker that automate rule tracking. Not using them is like trading without a chart.

10. Going It Alone

Not getting feedback or using tracking tools. Join communities, use the Telegram bot, and track everything.
