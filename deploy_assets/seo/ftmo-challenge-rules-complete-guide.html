Critical Rules Explained

Drawdown Calculation

FTMO calculates drawdown from your peak account balance, not your starting balance. This is the most misunderstood rule.

Example: Start $50,000 → Peak $55,000 → Drop to $52,000
❌ Wrong: ($52k - $50k) / $50k = 4% drawdown
✅ Correct: ($55k - $52k) / $55k = 5.45% drawdown

Daily Loss Limit

The daily loss limit is calculated from the previous day's ending balance, not your starting balance. For a 2-Step challenge with $50,000: your daily loss limit is 5% of the previous day's close.

Best Day Rule (1-Step Only)

Your most profitable trading day cannot exceed 50% of your total profit. If your best day was $3,000, you need total profit of at least $6,000 before you can pass.

Minimum Trading Days

2-Step challenges require minimum 4 trading days in each phase. This is a calendar-based requirement — each day counts, regardless of profit.

Common Rule Violations

  • Drawdown miscalculation: Using starting balance instead of peak balance
  • Daily loss overage: Not accounting for the previous day's balance
  • Best day rule: Having one exceptional day without enough total profit
  • Insufficient trading days: Rushing through without meeting minimums
