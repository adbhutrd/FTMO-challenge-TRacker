FTMO Trading Psychology: Mental Strategies

Most FTMO challenges are lost before the trader even opens a trade — not because of strategy, but because of psychology.

The Psychological Challenges of FTMO

1. Pressure to Perform

Knowing you're being evaluated creates pressure. This leads to overtrading, chasing losses, and abandoning your plan.

2. Drawdown Anxiety

When you see drawdown increasing, the natural instinct is to take bigger risks to recover. This usually makes things worse.

3. Profit Target Rush

As you approach the profit target, you might rush to close the challenge. This is when most mistakes happen.

4. Phase 2 Complacency

After passing Phase 1, some traders relax too much and take unnecessary risks in Phase 2.
