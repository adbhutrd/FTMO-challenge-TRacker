FTMO Max Drawdown: Complete Guide

The 10% max drawdown rule is the most failed FTMO rule. Here's exactly how it works, with examples and common mistakes.

How FTMO Drawdown Works

FTMO's max drawdown is 10% of your peak account balance. This means:

  • If your balance only goes down, drawdown is from starting balance
  • If you make profit and then lose, drawdown is from the highest point
  • The drawdown limit is STATIC (always 10% of starting balance) — but it's calculated from your PEAK

Critical Insight

You can fail even while being profitable overall. In Scenario 2 above, the trader is at break-even ($50k from $50k start) but has 9.1% drawdown because their peak was $55k. One more bad day and they fail — despite not losing any money overall.

How to Avoid Drawdown Failure

  • Track drawdown from peak, not starting balance
  • Use our free FTMO tracker for automatic drawdown calculation
  • If drawdown exceeds 5%, reduce position sizes
  • Never increase risk to recover from drawdown
